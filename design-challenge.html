Design Challenge

Using a worksheet from the Human Centered Design Toolkit, we determined what our design challenge would be for the non-profit we chose.

What is the problen you're trying to solve?

How can the Slow Food Upstate website help educate people and businesses about healthy food alternatives?

Now, state the ultimate impact you're trying to have.

To help farmers and consumers alike learn about the "slow food" movement and offer resources for them to share and buy.

What are some possible solutions to your problem?

Hierarchy, clear and concise information, more visible volunteer and membership options, and overall cleaner site navigation.

Finally, write down some of the content and constraints that you're facing.

Some demographics are too busy to stop and learn about slow food/buy from local farmers; food markets generally end in the late fall; the website needs to be navigable for the older demographic.

Does your original question need a tweak? Try it again.

How can the Slow Food Upstate website target a varied demographic (some with fast-paced lifestyles) in order to educate them about how to grow and sell/where to buy healthier food options?

The Design Challenge was helpful in directing my focus. With an action-based question, I was able to better determine what I should concentrate on during the redesign process to create a more effective website.
