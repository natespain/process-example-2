Audience and Goals

After conducting preliminary research, we determined who our audiences were and the goals applicable to those audiences.

Audience

In order to make the website redesign as effective and efficient as possible, the next step was to figure out who the target audiences were for the site. We started by using upside-down triangle thinking: start broad and then work towards specifics.

First, I recognized what the three main goals I believe Slow Food Upstate communicated. I did not establish these goals so that I could match them to my audience, but they served as more of loose guidlines and overarching categories.

After determining those broad goals, I made categories: Audience (as an Organization), Audience (Consumers/Earth Market), Sponsors, Partners, and Skeptics. I then proceded to fit a wide range and variety of audiences into each category.

I soon found that there were some audiences that fit into multiple categories. I wanted to cover all my bases, so I wedged them between columns to show interchangability. It was hard narrowing them down even further and deciding which section of the determined audience would fit best.

When it came down to refining my audience in this exercise, I focused on the categories Audience (as an Organization) and Audience (Consumers/Earth Market). I found that these two categories directly represented the main audience I wanted to reach.

Once the audience for the organization were specified, we could start defining applicable goals.

Goals

Overarching Goals

Client
  • Gain more memberships to support the organization.
  • Spread the word about who they are and what they do.
  • Raise awareness about the slow food movement throughout the upstate.
  • Connect with grocery stores and markets to distribute farmers' and growers' produce.
  • Connect with educators and schools to educate children and young adults about the slow food movement/healthy eating.
  • Be present in the community.
Audience
  • Aquire monetary support in the form of memberships and donations.
  • Inform the public about the slow food movement.
  • Provide easy methods to pursue a slow food lifestyle.
  • Have events/opportunities available to attend.
  • Have educational resources available for public use.
  • Reach out to possible volunteers.
  • Provide contact information for farmers/growers to reach the organization.
Website
  • Present membership information in an appealing and accessible fashion.
  • Provide a clear explanation of the slow food movement and what Slow Food Upstate does.
  • Provide recipies, ways the user can support/pursue a slow food lifestyle.
  • Have event information visible and accessible.
  • Have educational materials accessible and easy to download/use with little to no troubleshooting.
  • Have contact information available for suppliers/vendors.

Specific Goals

User Goal Content/Action
Young Families Information distribution about events, ways to further support/get involved, goals, the slow food movement itself, and updates in the organization. Email newsletter, donate, memberships, volunteer opportunities, resources, family-friendly events, and information about the slow food movement.
Young Couples Promotion of healthier eating, slow food movement lifestyle, and volunteer opportunities. Email newsletter, donate, memberships, volunteer opportunities, resources, and information about the slow food movement.
Educators/Schools Educational resources and event information. Host a speaker at the school, educational material, and accessible media.
Chefs/Farm-to-Table Restaurants Information distribution about farmers' markets and farmers/growers open to partnership. Email newsletter, host a chef/speaker who is a slow food advocate, and contact information to get in touch with someone who could further contact them to farmers/growers.
Farmers/Growers Provide contact information and ways to get involved/sell their produce. Contact information and a specific link for vendors/farmers/growers.

Conclusion and Final Thoughts

The primary overarching goals of the Slow Food Upstate website redesign is to boost membership, clarify the organization's mission and provide events/event information for the public.

Preliminary research of Slow Food Upstate - both primary and secondary - was much more helpful than I would have thought. This portion of the research was conducted before the interviews, so having a foundation of in-depth research about the organization really helped in defining goals and audiences.
